Project Proposal

To apply evolutionary machine learning algorithms to the problem of creating an autonomous vehicle in a simulated enviroment.

Research Questions

Can evolutionary learning be applied successfully to self driving vehicles?
Will neuroevolution drive the future of autonomous vehicles?
Could evolutionary hyperparameter optimization reduce the training requirements of advanced autonomous vehicle AI?

Thesis

By applying neuroevolutionary augmenting topologies to a simulated self driving agent. The training hardware requirements and model size will be drastically reduced.



Evolving Neural Networks through Augmenting Topologies

Contrasts fixed topology networks with evolving ones. Claims increased performance is based on speciation to protect innovation in structure and incremental structure evolution. Broad overview of NEATs, related data structures and experimental findings on widely used datasets.



Lamarckian Evolution of Convolutional Neural Networks

Lamarckian evolution of convolutional neural networks, assumes convolutional networks are best for image classification. Contains tons of information on the shortcomings of traditional NEATS and how they can be improved with higher functioning neurons to produce better results. Presents promising findings on the viability of NEATs with convolutions for image classification applications. Also describes evolutionary niching, where initially worse solutions are saved and continued to be evolved and evaluated again.



End to End Learning for Self-Driving Cars

Complete description of techniques and models used by Nvidia to create a simple fully autonomous vehicle using supervised data of specialized routes. Presents promising accuracy results for the technique used.



Introduction to Evolutionary Algorithms

Provides a complete indepth look at evolutionary learning techniques including speciacion, niching, mutation operators, and computational space required to execute this style of learning in a reasonable amount of time.
